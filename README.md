## Two Thinkers. One Phenomenon. Different Depths.

In 1962, Thomas Kuhn published *The Structure of Scientific Revolutions*. It became one of the most cited academic books of the twentieth century. It gave the world the concept of the paradigm shift. It described — with remarkable precision — why scientific progress does not happen smoothly, why new ideas are resisted, and why the resistance is not irrational.

Kuhn was doing phenomenology. He was describing what he could observe in the history of science. He could see the pattern. He could name it. He could document it across physics, chemistry, astronomy, and medicine.

What he could not do was say *why* — in precise mathematical terms — the pattern exists.

The Morrison Framework™ is that precision.
